## Step 1: Gather information

Read CLAUDE.md — genre, characters, structure, style.

Then search online (use WebSearch):
- Best examples of the genre: which films/series are the benchmarks? Why do they work?
- Genre conventions: what does the audience expect? What cannot be broken?
- Structural models for this genre: three-act, five-act, episodic?
- Dramatic systems of the genre: what drives the story (fear? absurdity? a moral question?)
- Anti-examples: what makes films in this genre fail?

## Step 2: Build compass_artifact.md

The file must contain **4-8 genre systems**, each in this format:

```
## N. System name

What it is and why it matters for the genre. 2-3 paragraphs with examples from benchmark films.

System rule: one clear statement — the rule every scene must follow.

For [PROJECT NAME]: specific application to this screenplay.
```

Required systems for most genres:
- Hook / inciting incident
- Story engine (central conflict or misconception)
- Escalation (how stakes grow)
- Structural model (with timing of loops/acts)
- Character arcs
- Tone and style (where the genre boundary is)
- Ending / bathos / catharsis
