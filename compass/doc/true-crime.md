# True Crime Documentary

> Compiled from StudioBinder, PremiumBeat, Wikipedia genre analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

Factual retelling of real criminal cases. The investigation is structured as a narrative — crime, investigation, trial, aftermath. The audience is positioned as detective. Ethical tension between entertainment and respect for victims is genre-defining.

---

## Benchmarks

*The Thin Blue Line* (1988, Morris) — falsely convicted man; re-investigation through film; *Making a Murderer* (Netflix, 2015), *The Jinx* (2015, HBO), *Paradise Lost* trilogy (1996–2011), *Amanda Knox* (2016), *Don't F**k with Cats* (Netflix, 2019)

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Crime introduction** — the act, victim, circumstances; often at maximum stakes
2. **Investigation sequence** — evidence, suspects, false leads; audience can theorize
3. **Trial or prosecution** — institutional response; procedural tension
4. **Aftermath** — what happened to all parties; justice assessed
5. **Interviews with survivors / investigators / perpetrators** — multiple perspectives, conflicting truths
6. **Re-enactment** — illustrating what cameras didn't capture; raises authenticity questions

---

## Ethical conventions

- Victim-centered framing is now genre expectation
- Perpetrator glamorization is a known risk to manage
- The film may change legal outcomes — responsibility is part of the genre

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Investigative Documentary:** true crime is retrospective; investigative is active
- **vs. Portrait Documentary:** true crime is about a case; portrait is about a person
