# Essay Film

> Compiled from Wikipedia, MasterClass, PremiumBeat documentary mode analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

Hybrid genre: the filmmaker's personal reflection combining images, text, and ideas. No obligatory narrative plot. The essayist thinks on screen — associatively, digressing, contradicting themselves. The "I" of the filmmaker is the subject.

---

## Benchmarks

*Sans Soleil* (1983, Marker), *The Gleaners and I* (2000, Varda), *F for Fake* (1973, Welles), *Waltz with Bashir* (2008, Folman), *13th* (2016, DuVernay), *Videocracy* (2009), *Baraka* (1992)

**Soviet/Russian:** *The Mirror* (*Зеркало*, 1975, Tarkovsky) — autobiographical memory as essay film; *Aleksei German's* late work; Loznitsa's essay documentaries

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Authorial voice** — the narrator is the filmmaker; "I" is the structural subject
2. **Associative structure** — images, archive, music juxtaposed by emotion and thought, not chronology
3. **Philosophical digression** — the film follows an idea wherever it leads
4. **Archive + new footage** — past materials reframed by present observation
5. **Self-awareness of the medium** — the film thinks about its own making
6. **No obligation to conclude** — the essay can end mid-thought; ambiguity is the point

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Poetic Documentary:** essay film has an arguing "I"; poetic documentary has only images
- **vs. Portrait Documentary:** essay film is about the filmmaker's thought process; portrait is about a subject
- **vs. Expository:** essay film questions; expository explains
