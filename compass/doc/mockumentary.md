# Mockumentary

> Compiled from Wikipedia, Dartmouth, StudioBinder genre analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

Fiction film in the form of a documentary. Usually comedy or satire. The fictional subjects behave as if real; the crew behaves as if filming real events. The gap between the documentary form and fictional content is the joke — or the horror.

---

## Benchmarks

*This Is Spinal Tap* (1984, Reiner) — the founding text; *Best in Show* (2000, Guest), *Man Bites Dog* (1992) — dark subversion; *The Office* (TV, 2001–), *What We Do in the Shadows* (2014), *Borat* (2006), *Behind the Mask: The Rise of Leslie Vernon* (2006)

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Talking heads** — interviews to camera; character-reveals through self-description
2. **Handheld cinema-vérité aesthetic** — shaky camera, available light as authenticity signal
3. **No music score** — silence and diegetic sound only
4. **The crew acknowledged** — characters talk to the camera; fourth wall is structural
5. **Banal subject treated with documentary gravity** — the incongruity of scale is the comedy
6. **"Found footage" framing** — may include text cards contextualizing the discovered footage

---

## Distinct from found-footage horror

Mockumentary is primarily comedic/satiric; found-footage horror (Blair Witch, REC) uses the same form to generate fear rather than laughter.

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Documentary:** mockumentary is fiction; its subjects are not real
- **vs. Satire:** mockumentary uses form as the satirical tool; satire can use any form
