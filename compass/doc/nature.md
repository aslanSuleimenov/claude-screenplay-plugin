# Nature / Wildlife Documentary

> Compiled from MasterClass, PremiumBeat, Wikipedia genre analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

Flora, fauna, ecosystems. The dominant style is the Attenborough television tradition: authoritative narration, spectacular footage, personified animal stories. Increasingly includes explicit ecological commentary.

---

## Benchmarks

*Planet Earth* (BBC, 2006), *Microcosmos* (1996), *Winged Migration* (2001), *The Cove* (2009), *My Octopus Teacher* (2020), *March of the Penguins* (2005), *Sweetgrass* (2009)

---

## Structural conventions

1. **"Voice of God" narration** — authoritative, knowledgeable, emotionally warm; Attenborough as template
2. **Animal personification** — individual animals given names, families, personal stories
3. **Seasonal / cyclical structure** — birth, survival, death, reproduction
4. **Extended field production** — months or years of footage compressed into dramatic sequences
5. **Ecological message** — human impact as frame, increasingly in contemporary docs
6. **Spectacular imagery** — the visual event (migration, predator kill, extreme weather) as payoff

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Observational Documentary:** nature doc uses voiceover and music heavily; observational does not
- **vs. Portrait Documentary:** nature doc's subject cannot consent or speak; portrait's can
- **vs. Investigative:** nature doc observes; investigative exposes
