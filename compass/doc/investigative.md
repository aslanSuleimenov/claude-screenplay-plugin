# Investigative Documentary

> Compiled from MasterClass, PremiumBeat, Wikipedia documentary mode analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

Documentary investigation: exposing hidden truth, corruption, or injustice. The investigation is the narrative spine. The filmmaker is a journalist; the film is the evidence. Risk — legal, personal, physical — is part of the story.

---

## Benchmarks

*Citizenfour* (2014, Poitras), *Icarus* (2017), *The Act of Killing* (2012, Oppenheimer), *Enron: The Smartest Guys in the Room* (2005), *The Thin Blue Line* (1988, Morris), *I Am Not Your Negro* (2016), *Bowling for Columbine* (2002)

**Soviet/Russian:** *The Term* (*Срок*, 2014, Kostyrev/Loznitsa) — political documentary investigation

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Investigation arc as narrative spine** — crime/injustice → evidence gathering → confrontation → (partial) revelation
2. **Anonymous sources and access problems** — the difficulty of access is part of the story
3. **Evidence accumulation** — documents, interviews, and footage build a case
4. **The "gotcha" moment** — confronting the subject with evidence on camera
5. **Legal risks as dramatic element** — what can and cannot be shown
6. **Partial resolution** — investigative docs rarely achieve full justice; the film is the accounting

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Expository Documentary:** investigative uncovers what's hidden; expository explains what's known
- **vs. Participatory Documentary:** investigative's director is investigator, not participant
- **vs. True Crime:** true crime is retrospective; investigative is often active and ongoing
