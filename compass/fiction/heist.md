# Heist

> Compiled from Wikipedia, MasterClass, StudioBinder genre analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

Planning, execution, and aftermath of one major criminal operation. A team of specialists with unique skills. The pleasure is the plan itself — the audience wants to see how it works, then how it goes wrong.

---

## Benchmarks

*Rififi* (1955, Dassin), *The Italian Job* (1969), *Heat* (1995), *Ocean's Eleven* (2001), *Inside Man* (2006), *The Town* (2010), *Thief* (1981), *Killing* (1956, Kubrick)

**Soviet/Russian:** *Gentlemen of Fortune* (*Джентльмены удачи*, 1971) — Soviet heist-comedy; *The Diamond Arm* elements; post-Soviet: *Zhmurki* (2005, Balabanov) — crime-comedy

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Team assembly** — each member has unique, irreplaceable function; recruitment sequence
2. **The plan laid out in detail** — audience sees the plan before execution; anticipation built
3. **Unexpected complication at execution** — the plan cannot survive contact with reality
4. **The double-cross** — trusted member betrays; or someone outside disrupts
5. **Final reveal of "how it actually happened"** — restructured timeline exposes the real plan
6. **The score as moral question** — what is the heist for, beyond money?

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Crime/Gangster:** heist is one job; gangster is a life in crime
- **vs. Thriller:** heist's tension is procedural; thriller's is existential
- **vs. Caper (lighter subtype):** caper is comedic; heist can be tragic or deadly serious
