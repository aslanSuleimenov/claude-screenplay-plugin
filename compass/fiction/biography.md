# Biography / Biopic

> Compiled from IMDB, MasterClass, StudioBinder genre analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

Nonfictional (or pseudo-nonfictional) story about a real person. The biopic chooses a "key period" — not a whole life, but a transformational span. Tension between historical fact and narrative necessity is the genre's central creative problem.

---

## Benchmarks

*Lawrence of Arabia* (1962), *Amadeus* (1984, fictionalizing Salieri's perspective), *Raging Bull* (1980), *I, Tonya* (2017), *Oppenheimer* (2023), *The Social Network* (2010), *Capote* (2005), *Lincoln* (2012)

**Soviet/Russian:** *Admiral* (2008, Kravchuk) — Kolchak; *Tsar* (2009, Lungin) — Ivan the Terrible; *Lenin in October* (1937) — Soviet hagiographic biopic as genre study; *Eisenstein in Guanajuato* (2015, Greenaway)

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Selection of key period** — not birth-to-death; one crisis, one achievement, one fall
2. **Public vs. private self as conflict** — the gap between legend and person is the story
3. **Transformational moment as narrative center** — the hinge on which the life turns
4. **Compression and composite characters** — real events restructured for narrative coherence
5. **Epilogue with real outcomes** — text card anchors fiction to historical record
6. **The question of sympathy** — audience must understand even unsympathetic subjects

---

## Structural risks

- **Hagiography** — uncritical celebration destroys tension
- **Cradle-to-grave structure** — no dramatic arc; everything is equally important
- **Over-explanation** — real people rarely explain themselves; let behavior speak
- **Impersonation trap** — performance mimicry replaces character understanding

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Historical Epic:** biopic centers on one consciousness; epic centers on events
- **vs. Drama:** biopic carries the weight of real events and real consequences
