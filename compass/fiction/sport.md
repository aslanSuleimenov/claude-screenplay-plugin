## Definition

AFI: "protagonists are involved in athletics or competitive games." Utopian logic: hard work + rules = success. Sport as arena for examining identity, class, race, body, failure. The final match is the genre's climax — but the real story is always off the field.

## Benchmarks

*Rocky* (1976), *Raging Bull* (1980), *Moneyball* (2011), *Hoop Dreams* (1994, documentary), *Rush* (2013), *The Wrestler* (2008), *Bull Durham* (1988), *Whiplash* (2014)

**Soviet/Russian:** *Goalkeeper of the Galaxy* (*Вратарь Галактики*); *Penalty Kick* (*Одиннадцать надежд*, 1975) — football drama; *Legenda №17* (2013) — hockey biopic (Kharlamov)

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Training sequence as narrative center** — the preparation is where character is formed
2. **Final match / race / competition as climax** — everything converges in one performance
3. **The mentor and their limitation** — coach or trainer who cannot complete the journey with the hero
4. **Personal flaw overcome through sport** — the athletic achievement is also a psychological one
5. **Tension between sport and life outside the arena** — family, money, health, identity
6. **The body as dramatic subject** — injury, peak, decline, recovery

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Action:** sport film's physical confrontation has rules and institutional context
- **vs. Biopic:** sport biopic combines both; sport film may be fictional
- **vs. Drama:** sport film provides external structure (competition) that drama lacks
