# Crime / Gangster

> Compiled from IMDB, Letterboxd, AFI, Wikipedia genre analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

Centers on organized crime or individual criminals. AFI: "centers on organized crime or the gangster as an individual criminal in the context of society." The classic arc: rise through crime → inevitable fall / betrayal.

---

## Benchmarks

*The Godfather* (1972), *GoodFellas* (1990), *Scarface* (1983), *City of God* (2002), *Heat* (1995), *Reservoir Dogs* (1992), *The Departed* (2006), *Memories of Murder* (2003)

**Soviet/Russian:** *Brother* (*Брат*, 1997, Balabanov) — post-Soviet crime mythmaking; *Cargo 200* (*Груз 200*, 2007, Balabanov) — Soviet-era crime as horror; *The Meeting Place Cannot Be Changed* (*Место встречи изменить нельзя*, 1979, Govorukhin) — Soviet detective landmark; *Gentlemen of Fortune* (*Джентльмены удачи*, 1971) — crime-comedy

## Structural conventions

1. **Rise to power through crime** — early success is seductive; the audience is meant to be attracted
2. **Inevitable fall** — the genre demands payment; crime doesn't pay is the moral engine
3. **Family vs. organization as competing loyalties** — the personal destroyed by the professional
4. **Code of honor inside an amoral system** — the criminal has rules; betrayal of them is tragedy
5. **Attractive lifestyle, repulsive consequences** — style and violence coexist
6. **Betrayal from within** — the fatal blow comes from a trusted ally

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Thriller:** crime centers on the criminal's world; thriller centers on the detective/victim's perspective
- **vs. Noir:** noir uses the detective as protagonist; crime/gangster uses the criminal
- **vs. Heist:** heist is about one specific job; crime is about a life in crime
