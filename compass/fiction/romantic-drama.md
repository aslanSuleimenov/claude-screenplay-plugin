# Romantic Drama

> Compiled from Letterboxd, MasterClass, Wikipedia genre analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

Conflict generated by romantic relationships, without the obligatory comic resolution. Focus on emotional depth and loss. The obstacle may be insurmountable — class, time, death, circumstance. Tragedy is permitted.

---

## Benchmarks

*Brief Encounter* (1945, Lean), *Blue Valentine* (2010), *Call Me by Your Name* (2017), *Portrait of a Lady on Fire* (2019), *Carol* (2015), *In the Mood for Love* (2000, Wong Kar-wai), *Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind* (2004), *Atonement* (2007)

**Soviet/Russian:** *Moscow Does Not Believe in Tears* (*Москва слезам не верит*, 1980, Menshov) — romantic drama, Oscar winner; *Autumn Marathon* (*Осенний марафон*, 1979, Danelia); *The Cranes Are Flying* (*Летят журавли*, 1957) — wartime romance as tragedy; *A Cruel Romance* (*Жестокий романс*, 1984, Ryazanov)

## Structural conventions

1. **Irremovable obstacle** — the thing keeping lovers apart is structural, not a misunderstanding
2. **The past as wound** — history of each character generates the present conflict
3. **Both partners are complex and sympathetic** — no villain; only incompatibility or circumstance
4. **Open or tragic ending permitted** — resolution through loss is valid; genre does not demand reunion
5. **Slow exposition of feeling** — emotion revealed through accumulation, not declaration
6. **The body as emotional register** — touch, distance, looks, absence speak more than dialogue

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Romantic Comedy:** romcom requires humor and happy resolution; romantic drama neither
- **vs. Melodrama:** melodrama exaggerates; romantic drama aims for psychological realism
- **vs. Drama:** romantic drama's conflict is relational; general drama's conflict is broader
