## Definition

Large-scale armed conflict between opposing forces in a world with the same physical laws as ours. War film uses combat as the lens for examining human nature, institutional machinery, and moral cost. Two poles: heroic (war is redemptive) and anti-war (war is catastrophe).

---

## Benchmarks

*Paths of Glory* (1957, Kubrick), *Apocalypse Now* (1979), *Full Metal Jacket* (1987), *Dunkirk* (2017), *Saving Private Ryan* (1998), *The Thin Red Line* (1998), *Das Boot* (1981), *M\*A\*S\*H* (1970)

**Soviet (essential):** *Come and See* (*Иди и смотри*, 1985, Klimov) — the definitive anti-war film, sensory immersion in horror; *The Cranes Are Flying* (*Летят журавли*, 1957, Kalatozov) — WWII from the home front; *Ivan's Childhood* (*Иваново детство*, 1962, Tarkovsky); *The Ascent* (*Восхождение*, 1977, Shepitko) — moral choice under occupation; *In the Trenches of Stalingrad* (*Они сражались за Родину*, 1975, Bondarchuk)

## Structural conventions

1. **Democratic ensemble** — mix of backgrounds, ranks, personalities as unit
2. **Leader who must separate from group** — command as isolation
3. **Specific tactical objective** — the mission gives structure; survival is the counter-objective
4. **Personalized enemy** — at least one face on the other side
5. **Cost accumulation** — each advance destroys something irreplaceable
6. **Technology and costume as authenticity markers** — period accuracy is genre contract with the audience

---

## Subtypes

- **Combat film** — front-line action as primary setting
- **Anti-war** — exposes the machinery of dehumanization; irony, horror, absurdism
- **Home front** — war's effect on civilians, families, women
- **Prisoner of war** — confinement, escape, identity under pressure
- **War absurdism** — *Dr. Strangelove*, *Catch-22*, *M\*A\*S\*H*

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Action:** war requires institutional/political context; action is individual
- **vs. Historical Epic:** war film focuses on combat experience; epic focuses on scale and consequence
- **vs. Thriller:** war film's threat is systemic and shared; thriller's is targeted at one protagonist
