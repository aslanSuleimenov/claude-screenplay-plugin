## Definition

Films defined by physical stakes and risk — chases, gunfights, explosions, hand-to-hand combat. Conflict is resolved physically, not psychologically. Pace is the primary tool.

---

## Benchmarks

*Die Hard* (1988), *Mad Max: Fury Road* (2015), *The Raid* (2011), *John Wick* (2014), *Speed* (1994), *Terminator 2* (1991), *Heat* (1995), *Aliens* (1986)

**Soviet/Russian:** *Brother* (*Брат*, 1997, Balabanov), *Brother 2* (2000), *The Diamond Arm* (*Бриллиантовая рука*, 1969, Gaidai — action-comedy)

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Protagonist with exceptional physical or tactical skills** — ordinary person thrust into extraordinary circumstances, or trained professional
2. **Clearly named threat / villain** — motivation matters less than presence
3. **Escalating stakes** toward a climactic confrontation — each set piece raises the cost
4. **Minimal psychological interiority** — character revealed through action, not reflection
5. **Set piece architecture** — film structured around 3–5 escalating action sequences
6. **Physical consequence** — wounds, exhaustion, resource depletion accumulate

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Thriller:** action resolves conflict physically; thriller resolves it through information/psychology
- **vs. Adventure:** action focuses on combat; adventure focuses on journey and discovery
- **vs. War:** action hero is singular; war genre demands ensemble and institutional context
