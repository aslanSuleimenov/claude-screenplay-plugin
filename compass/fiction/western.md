# Western

> Compiled from IMDB, Letterboxd, AFI, Wikipedia genre analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

AFI: "films set in the American West that embody the spirit, struggle, and demise of the new frontier." The Western is a morality play: civilization vs. wilderness, law vs. anarchy. The landscape is an active participant, not background.

---

## Benchmarks

**Classic:** *Stagecoach* (1939, Ford), *The Searchers* (1956, Ford), *High Noon* (1952), *Shane* (1953), *Rio Bravo* (1959)

**Spaghetti:** *The Good, the Bad and the Ugly* (1966, Leone), *Once Upon a Time in the West* (1968, Leone), *Django* (1966)

**Revisionist / Neo-Western:** *Unforgiven* (1992), *McCabe & Mrs. Miller* (1971), *The Wild Bunch* (1969), *No Country for Old Men* (2007), *Hell or High Water* (2016), *Wind River* (2017)

**Soviet Eastern (Ostern):** *White Sun of the Desert* (*Белое солнце пустыни*, 1970) — the Soviet Western canon; *At Home Among Strangers* (*Свой среди чужих*, 1974, Mikhalkov)

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Morality play** — law vs. anarchy; civilization vs. nature; honor vs. expediency
2. **Lone wanderer / cowboy / sheriff** — arrives from outside; restores or destroys order; leaves
3. **Town as fragile outpost of order** — surrounded by wilderness; one man holds it together
4. **Duel as narrative climax** — face-to-face confrontation; code of honor
5. **Landscape as character** — desert, mountains, sky as moral geography
6. **The price of civilization** — progress destroys what made the West heroic

---

## Subtypes

- **Classic Western** — clear moral universe; hero prevails
- **Spaghetti Western** — morally ambiguous; style as content; Leone's operatic scale
- **Revisionist Western** — myths dismantled; Native American perspective; female protagonists
- **Neo-Western** — Western themes in contemporary settings (suburbs, highways, drug trade)

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Action:** Western is a genre with a specific geography, mythology, and moral code
- **vs. Adventure:** Western's journey is homeward or toward moral confrontation, not discovery
