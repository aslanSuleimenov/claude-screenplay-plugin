# Mystery / Detective

> Compiled from IMDB, Letterboxd, AFI, MasterClass genre analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

AFI: "revolves around the solution of a crime." Intellectual genre: the detective and audience decode the puzzle in parallel. Crime is a disruption of order; detection restores it. The pleasure is cognitive, not visceral.

---

## Benchmarks

*The Maltese Falcon* (1941), *Chinatown* (1974), *Knives Out* (2019), *Memories of Murder* (2003), *Laura* (1944), *Anatomy of a Murder* (1959), *Rear Window* (1954), *Zodiac* (2007)

**Soviet:** *The Meeting Place Cannot Be Changed* (*Место встречи изменить нельзя*, 1979, Govorukhin) — definitive Soviet detective; *The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes and Dr. Watson* (TV, 1979–86) — canonical adaptation; *Dead Season* (*Мёртвый сезон*, 1968)

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Crime as disruption at the opening** — murder, theft, disappearance shatters a world
2. **Field of suspects with motive and alibi** — audience can theorize along with detective
3. **Red herrings and metered clues** — information withheld, then revealed strategically
4. **Eccentric detective or outsider** — insight comes from being different from the crowd
5. **Revelation restores order** — naming the killer/solution closes the disruption
6. **The detective's method as character** — deduction, intuition, or obsession defines who they are

---

## Detective types

- **Professional investigator** — police procedural; institutional constraints
- **Amateur/civilian** — often comedy-inflected; Agatha Christie template
- **Private detective** — operates outside institutions; moral flexibility

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Thriller:** mystery withholds information from the audience; thriller often shares it with them
- **vs. Crime/Gangster:** mystery follows the detective; crime follows the criminal
- **vs. Noir:** mystery resolves order; noir reveals order was an illusion
