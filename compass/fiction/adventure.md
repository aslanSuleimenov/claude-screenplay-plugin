# Adventure

> Compiled from IMDB, Letterboxd, AFI, MasterClass, StudioBinder genre analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

Defined by the journey — physical or metaphorical — toward a goal. Any setting (historical, fantasy, exotic). Central question: will the hero reach the destination? Discovery matters as much as arrival.

---

## Benchmarks

*Raiders of the Lost Ark* (1981), *The Treasure of the Sierra Madre* (1948), *Lawrence of Arabia* (1962), *127 Hours* (2010), *Into the Wild* (2007), *The Princess Bride* (1987), *Aguirre, the Wrath of God* (1972), *Fitzcarraldo* (1982)

**Soviet:** *The Adventures of Buratino* (1975), *White Sun of the Desert* (*Белое солнце пустыни*, 1970) — iconic Soviet adventure, road-movie hybrid

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Quest as structural spine** — the goal must be clear and the route uncertain
2. **Exotic or inaccessible locus** — the destination defines the genre's promise
3. **Obstacles requiring ingenuity, not only force** — problem-solving over combat
4. **Companions / ensemble** — the team as microcosm; loyalty tested
5. **Discovery of self** — external journey mirrors internal transformation
6. **The world is larger and stranger than the hero expected**

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Action:** adventure emphasizes journey and discovery; action emphasizes combat
- **vs. Road Movie:** adventure has a defined goal; road movie values the wandering itself
- **vs. Quest Fantasy:** adventure is grounded in plausible reality; fantasy adds magic systems
