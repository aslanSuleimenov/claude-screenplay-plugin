# Spy / Espionage

> Compiled from Wikipedia, MasterClass, No Film School genre analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

Covert operations, international intrigue, hidden identities. Two poles: glamorous action (Bond model) and paranoid realism (le Carré model). The spy is defined by performance — they are never entirely themselves.

---

## Benchmarks

**Glamorous:** *North by Northwest* (1959), *Dr. No* (1962), *Mission: Impossible* (1996), *Kingsman* (2014)

**Realistic/Paranoid:** *The Spy Who Came in from the Cold* (1965), *Tinker Tailor Soldier Spy* (2011), *Three Days of the Condor* (1975), *The Lives of Others* (2006), *Munich* (2005)

**Soviet:** *Dead Season* (*Мёртвый сезон*, 1968) — Soviet spy film template; *TASS Is Authorized to Announce...* (TV, 1984) — KGB procedural; *Seventeen Moments of Spring* (*Семнадцать мгновений весны*, 1973, Lioznova) — the definitive Soviet spy series; *Resident's Error* (*Ошибка резидента*, 1968)

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Double agents and unclear loyalties** — who works for whom is never certain
2. **Exotic locations** — geography as aspiration and danger
3. **Technology / tradecraft** — gadgets (glamorous) or dead drops and surveillance (realistic)
4. **The mission that reveals a greater conspiracy** — the immediate task opens into something larger
5. **The cost of the work on the human being** — what is sacrificed for operational success
6. **Identity as performance** — the spy cannot reveal themselves; alienation is structural

---

## Subtypes

- **Action-spy (Bond model):** clear villain, spectacular set pieces, impervious hero
- **Paranoid thriller (le Carré model):** institutions betray the agent; the enemy may be inside
- **Cold War spy:** ideological confrontation; double agents; defections

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Action:** spy film requires institutional/political context; action needs only a threat
- **vs. Thriller:** spy thriller is a specific institutional setting; thriller is the mood
