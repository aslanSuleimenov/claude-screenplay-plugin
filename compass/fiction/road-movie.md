# Road Movie

> Compiled from Wikipedia, FilmReference, StudioBinder genre analysis.

Index: `../INDEX.md`

---

## Definition

Heroes leave "home" and travel. The open road as metaphor for freedom, alienation, and national identity. Unlike adventure, the road movie values the wandering itself — arrival may be irrelevant or impossible. The journey is the destination.

---

## Benchmarks

*Easy Rider* (1969), *Thelma & Louise* (1991), *Paris, Texas* (1984, Wenders), *Y Tu Mamá También* (2001), *Nebraska* (2013), *Five Easy Pieces* (1970), *The Straight Story* (1999), *Little Miss Sunshine* (2006)

**Soviet/Russian:** *White Sun of the Desert* (*Белое солнце пустыни*, 1970) — road-adventure hybrid; *Kin-dza-dza!* (1986) — absurdist road movie to another planet; *The Cuckoo* (*Кукушка*, 2002) — road-war hybrid; *Koktebel* (2003) — Russian road movie template

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Departure as rupture** — something at home is broken; the road is an escape from it
2. **Movement as narrative structure** — the journey dictates scene sequence
3. **Random encounters form the protagonist** — strangers encountered become the real story
4. **The partner as contrast** — companions differ from protagonist in ways that create conflict and growth
5. **"Home" revisited** — what they left is reconsidered by the end (returned to, escaped, or mourned)
6. **Landscape as national character** — the road reveals something about the country being crossed

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Adventure:** road movie values the wandering; adventure has a defined goal
- **vs. Coming-of-Age:** road movie's transformation is existential, not developmental
- **vs. Buddy Film:** buddy film can be stationary; road movie requires movement
