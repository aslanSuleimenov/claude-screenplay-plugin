## Definition

Genre whose primary goal is to provoke fear, dread, and revulsion. The threat may be supernatural or real; the stake is survival or sanity. Horror weaponizes what the audience already fears.

---

## Benchmarks

*The Shining* (1980), *Hereditary* (2018), *Get Out* (2017), *The Exorcist* (1973), *Rosemary's Baby* (1968), *The Witch* (2015), *Midsommar* (2019), *Suspiria* (1977)

**Soviet/Eastern European:** *Viy* (1967, USSR) — first Soviet horror film, folklore-based; *Stalker* (1979, Tarkovsky) — existential dread without genre conventions

---

## Structural conventions

1. **Isolated or vulnerable protagonist** — cut off from help before or during threat
2. **Escalating threat without full explanation** — the unknown is scarier than the revealed
3. **Violation of safe space** — home, body, or community becomes the source of danger
4. **Suspense through audience knowledge** — we know what the character doesn't
5. **The monster as metaphor** — rarely just a monster; encodes social anxiety

## Subtypes

### Slasher
Killer hunts group; Final Girl; mask / weapon as iconography; high body count, low psychological depth.
*Benchmarks:* Halloween (1978), Scream (1996), A Nightmare on Elm Street (1984)

### Psychological Horror
Threat is internal or ambiguous. Boundary between real and imagined is erased. Minimum violence, maximum dread.
*Benchmarks:* Black Swan (2010), The Babadook (2014), Repulsion (1965)

### Body Horror
Graphic transformation, degradation, or parasitism of the body. Fear of losing bodily autonomy. Cronenberg as founding auteur.
*Benchmarks:* The Fly (1986), Videodrome (1983), Titane (2021), Annihilation (2018)

### Folk Horror
Rural/isolated setting, pagan practices, clash of modern and archaic. Horror emanates from community, not monster.
*Benchmarks:* The Wicker Man (1973), The Witch (2015), Blood on Satan's Claw (1971)

---

## Distinction from similar genres

- **vs. Thriller:** horror threat is often irrational/supernatural; thriller threat is human and logical
- **vs. Psychological Thriller:** horror aims for visceral fear; thriller for intellectual suspense
